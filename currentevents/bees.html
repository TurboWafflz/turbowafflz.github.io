Bees Stopped Buzzing During Total Eclipse

Citizen scientists (mostly students) recorded the buzzing of bees suddenly stopping during the total eclipse in August 2017. The dimmed lights and slightly cooler temperatures didn't appear to affect the bees, but when the eclipse was total, they suddenly stopped buzzing. While they can't reliably tell what kinds of bees were buzzing, they estimate that sites in Missouri had a lot of bumblebees and western sites had Megachile bees. This is the first data published about bees reaction to a solar eclipse.
