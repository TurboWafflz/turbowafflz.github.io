Maple Syrup Urine Disease

 
By: Finian Wright


Maple syrup urine disease (also called BCKD deficiency, branched-chain alpha-keto acid dehydrogenase deficiency, branched-chain ketoaciduria, ketoacidemia, or MSUD) is a genetic disease that causes the body to be unable to produce certain amino acids correctly. It gets it's name because one of the first symptoms is urine with an odor similar to that of maple syrup. If untreated it can cause comas, seizures, and even death in people with the disease.



Maple syrup urine disease is caused by mutations in the BCKDHA, BCKDHB, or DBT genes.
