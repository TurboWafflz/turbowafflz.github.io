STARS-Me Mini space elevator

On September 22, the Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency sent the STARS-Me (Space Tethered Autonomous Robotic Satellite - Mini elevator) experiment into orbit. STARS-Me is made up of two 10cm3 satellites with a 10 meter long cable going between them, and a small 3cm*6cm elevator which moves along the cable. This is not the first satellite to test a tethered cable, but is however the first to test moving along the cable. This could be similar to the way a full size elevator could work in the future, although we do not currently have the technology to make one according to the Japan Aerospace Exploration Agency.
