Drone that flys like a fruit fly

The DelFly Nimble, created by researchers in the Netherlands, is a drone that mimics the movement of a fruit fly to fly in patterns that no other drone could, such as turning 90 degrees without losing much momentum. The drone was built to help study fruit flies, but it's flight capability means it could be a good commercial drone. The drone has four wings which flap at seventeen beats per second. It can fly in any direction with only small changes to it's wing motion. It can go about 25km/h and do tricks such as barrel rolls. The Nimble is the first drone MAVLab has built using only off the shelf components, and weighs only 29 grams.
